Pakistan's public debt saw a significant reduction, falling by over $4.86 billion (Rs1,371 billion) between July and September 2025. This marks the first quarterly decline since 2019 and represents the largest reduction in both absolute value and percentage terms.

In a move towards sustainable agriculture, HBL Microfinance Bank has begun issuing Pakistan's first climate-smart agriculture loans. These loans leverage satellite data through a partnership with SUPARCO to support technology-driven, climate-resilient farming practices.

The country's current account deficit surged by 255% to $733 million from July to October, a rise attributed to increased imports and a decline in exports. Despite this, auto financing has grown to Rs315 billion, driven by more affordable rates.

The stock market has experienced volatility, with the KSE-100 index closing lower by 248 points due to late selling pressure after initial gains.

Other notable financial news includes the repeated postponement of the 11th National Finance Commission's inaugural session due to ongoing disagreements over resource distribution, and Pakistan facing a potential $2 billion arbitration claim from Saudi and Kuwaiti investors concerning the K-Electric dispute. Islamic finance is also being highlighted for its potential to advance the climate agenda, despite existing institutional and policy challenges. Exports to Europe have increased to $3.2 billion, though foreign investment has simultaneously dropped by 26%. Additionally, PIA and Biman Bangladesh Airlines have signed a cargo agreement to enhance regional trade, and Pakistan has adjusted fuel prices by increasing the petrol levy and decreasing the diesel levy.
